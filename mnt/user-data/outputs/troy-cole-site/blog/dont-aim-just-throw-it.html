DON'T AIM IT —
JUST THROW IT

What my 9-year-old son's travel baseball team taught me about closing more consultations — and why overthinking is the #1 conversion killer.

As head coach for my 9-year-old son's travel baseball team, we emphasize a mental approach that has implications far beyond the baseball diamond.

We call it: "Don't aim it — just throw it."

When a young pitcher stands on the mound and tries to consciously aim every pitch, they tense up, overthink, and lose the natural mechanics they've been training. The solution isn't more aiming. It's less thinking.

The same thing happens on your phones every single day. Your scheduler starts "aiming" — thinking too hard about what to say next — and the natural, confident conversation falls apart.

WHY OVERTHINKING KILLS CONVERSIONS

I did a live coaching event recently and asked the team: "When is it hardest for you to stay in the moment?" The answers came fast:

  • "When someone asks about the price right away"
  • "When a patient is cold or annoyed on the phone"
  • "When I'm talking to someone about LASIK — I'm better with cataract"

In every case, the breakdown wasn't a knowledge problem. It was a presence problem. They were projecting into the future — worrying about objections they hadn't heard yet, preparing defenses for attacks that weren't coming — instead of simply being in the conversation.

THE FIX IS SIMPLER THAN YOU THINK

You can't script your way out of this. And you can't memorize your way out of it either. The answer is belief and reps.

Belief: If your team genuinely believes they're offering a life-changing service at the best practice in town, the conversation becomes easy. They're not selling — they're helping. That shift is everything.

Reps: The more your team has these conversations — and the more they're coached through them in real time — the more the scripts become internalized. Until they stop sounding like scripts at all.

This is exactly what we build in the E3 Conversion System. Not a binder of scripts. A set of internalized habits and beliefs that make every phone call feel natural.

THE CHALLENGE FOR YOUR TEAM

Ask your team this week: "When is it hardest for you to stay in the moment?"

The answers will tell you exactly where to focus your coaching. And once you know the moments where they're aiming instead of throwing, you can fix them — fast.

Remember: whether on the baseball field, at a recital, on the phone, or in a consult, the key to success is often not in aiming for perfection but in throwing yourself into the moment.
